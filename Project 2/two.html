BMW M3 2024

    Intro:

    The BMW M3 encapsulates a thrilling essence, starting with its base powertrain that unleashes 473 horsepower from a twin-turbocharged straight-six engine. This force is seamlessly transmitted to the rear wheels via a six-speed manual gearbox, ensuring rapid power delivery. But the excitement doesn't stop there. The M3 Competition variant escalates the thrill with 503 horsepower, accessible through an eight-speed automatic transmission. This model also provides the option between rear-wheel and all-wheel drive configurations, catering to different driving preferences. Surprisingly, despite its formidable power, the M3 demonstrates remarkable composure at lower speeds, offering a pleasing drive that belies its aggressive capabilities. In comparison, its competitors, such as the Cadillac CT4-V Blackwing and the Alfa Romeo Giulia Quadrifoglio, sometimes offer a more polished yet less spirited driving experience. Nonetheless, each of these high-performance sedans has earned the 2024 Editors' Choice awards, highlighting their exceptional quality and appeal. The M3 lineup is further enhanced by the introduction of the 2024 M3 CS, a lighter and more powerful version that drops an estimated 75 pounds from the base model and boasts an awe-inspiring 543 horsepower, coupled with all-wheel drive, for an unparalleled driving experience.


    Engine, Transmission, and Performance:

    Following in the footsteps of the new M4 coupe, the M3 sedan boasts a formidable twin-turbo 3.0-liter inline-six engine. The standard version propels with 473 horsepower and 408 pound-feet of torque, directed exclusively to the rear wheels. This version is paired with a six-speed manual transmission, underscoring BMW's commitment to driving purists. The M3 Competition takes performance a notch higher, offering 503 horsepower and 479 pound-feet of torque. Unlike the base model, the Competition variant is only available with an eight-speed automatic transmission. It comes standard with rear-wheel drive, but an innovative all-wheel-drive option is also on offer, which interestingly includes a rear-drive mode for a more engaging driving experience. At the pinnacle of the lineup is the M3 CS, a tour de force with 543 horsepower, all-wheel drive, and an exclusive eight-speed automatic transmission. It distinguishes itself with numerous carbon-fiber-reinforced plastic components, resulting in a weight reduction of 39 pounds compared to the M3 Competition xDrive, albeit it is 310 pounds heavier than the M4 CSL coupe. Every M3 model is equipped with adaptive dampers and a brake pedal feel that can be customized to the driver's preference. For those seeking even more formidable stopping power, the sedan can be upgraded with robust carbon-ceramic brakes, which boast striking gold-painted calipers. Our initial driving experiences with both the standard M3 and the Competition model revealed their exceptional capability for executing dramatic drifts, greatly aided by the optional M Drift Analyzer feature (included in the M Drive Professional package). The tactile satisfaction delivered by the manual gearbox, coupled with the engine's vigorous acceleration, left a lasting impression. However, despite these attributes, when juxtaposed with the intensely engaging Alfa Romeo Giulia Quadrifoglio, the M3 falls slightly short in providing an all-encompassing driver immersion.


    0-80 MPH Times:

    During extensive testing on our dedicated circuit, our enduring BMW M3, outfitted with a manual gearbox and propelled by rear-wheel drive, achieved a notable speed of 80 mph in just 3.9 seconds. In comparison, an M3 Competition model, enhanced with all-wheel drive and paired with a sophisticated eight-speed automatic transmission, showcased exceptional performance by accelerating to 80 mph in a mere 2.8 seconds. However, for those in pursuit of the pinnacle of acceleration, the elite M3 CS model stands unrivaled. With its advanced engineering and in the capable hands of our seasoned testing team, it astonishingly reached 80 mph from a standstill in an astounding 2.7 seconds, setting a new benchmark for speed and performance within the series.


    Fuel Economy and Real-World MPG:

    The M3 sedan, a standout model in its class, boasts fuel efficiency with ratings of up to 18 miles per gallon (mpg) in urban settings and 23 mpg on open highways. Opting for the all-wheel-drive variant slightly reduces its highway fuel efficiency to an estimated 22 mpg. However, the M3 sedan has demonstrated exceptional performance in real-world conditions, surpassing its EPA highway estimates. Specifically, it achieved an impressive 32 mpg on our highway test loop, which is a critical component of our thorough testing protocol. Furthermore, the high-performance 543-horsepower M3 CS model also showcased its fuel efficiency prowess by securing a 30 mpg rating during our highway fuel economy tests. To explore detailed information regarding the BMW M4's fuel efficiency and to compare it with other vehicles, you are encouraged to visit the EPA's official website.


    Interior, Comfort, and Cargo:

    Beyond the signature "M" badges and unique trim options that distinguish the M3 from the standard 3-series, the interior design of the M3 mirrors its less performance-focused sibling in layout and functionality. This similarity includes the overall design, space for passengers, and the clarity of view outside the vehicle. However, M3 models elevate the experience with superior interior materials and craftsmanship that not only match but often surpass those found in the top variants of the 3-series. The M3's cabin is enriched with an increased use of carbon fiber and microsuede embellishments, creating a more race-inspired atmosphere. The centerpiece for the driver is an expansive, curved digital gauge display, which transforms into the M View in the more aggressive driving modes. This is easily activated by striking red buttons on the M3's robust steering wheel. The M View mode enhances the driving experience by introducing a shift light indicator and replacing the standard tachometer with a version that prioritizes ease of readability. Moreover, the M3 offers exceptionally contoured, lightweight front seats that ensure remarkable lateral support, enhancing the sporty feel without compromising comfort. These seats come as standard on the Competition model. While they are designed to provide superior support during spirited driving, their comfort level may vary during extended travel periods.
