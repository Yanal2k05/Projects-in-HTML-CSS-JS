BMW M4 2024

    Intro:

    The BMW M4 coupe emerges as a powerhouse of performance, reminiscent of a mythical beast with its breathtaking capabilities. As the two-door counterpart to the iconic M3 sedan, this model has been meticulously enhanced by BMW's M performance division to deliver unparalleled driving excitement. It shares the M3's formidable 473-horsepower twin-turbocharged inline-six engine, a testament to BMW's engineering prowess. The commitment to driving purists is evident with the standard six-speed manual transmission and rear-wheel drive setup. For those seeking an even higher adrenaline rush, the Competition variant elevates the experience with 503 horsepower and transitions to an eight-speed automatic transmission, with the option to add all-wheel drive for enhanced dynamics. The M4's suspension is tuned for precision, making every twist and turn an exhilarating adventure, while its luxury-laden cabin offers a generous space for comfort. Despite its virtues, the M4's firm ride, nuanced steering feedback, and the complex nature of its configurable drive modes invite criticism, especially when compared to rivals such as the Cadillac CT4-V Blackwing, Mercedes-AMG C83, and Alfa Romeo Giulia Quadrifoglio, which may offer a more refined handling experience. Nevertheless, the M4's exceptional blend of performance and luxury secures its place as a 2024 Editors' Choice awardee.


    Engine, Transmission, and Performance:

    The BMW M4 is powered by an exceptional twin-turbocharged 3.0-liter inline-six engine, available in two levels of performance. The base model delivers a robust 473 horsepower and 408 pound-feet of torque, mated to a rear-wheel-drive system and a six-speed manual transmission. In performance testing, this configuration accelerated to 80 mph in just 3.8 seconds and completed the quarter-mile in 12 seconds at a speed of 121 mph. The more potent M4 Competition variant ups the ante with 503 horsepower and 479 pound-feet of torque, though it swaps the manual for an eight-speed automatic transmission. Unique to the Competition model is the option for a rear-biased all-wheel-drive system, which significantly enhances acceleration, enabling it to reach 80 mph 1 second faster than the standard model in test conditions, achieving this benchmark in an astonishing 2.8 seconds. Every M4 is equipped with sophisticated adaptive dampers, a brake pedal with adjustable feedback, and an electronically controlled exhaust system that amplifies the engine's roar in Sport and Sport Plus modes, which can be modulated with the M Sound button for quieter moments. While BMW offers a plethora of adjustable drive-mode settings, potentially overwhelming some drivers, the core driving experience remains exhilarating. Our testing of the M4 Competition highlighted its rapid acceleration, remarkable cornering capabilities, and unwavering stability. The standard M4 sits on 18-inch wheels at the front and 19-inch wheels at the rear, whereas the M4 Competition features a staggered setup with 19-inch wheels at the front and 20-inch wheels at the rear. A carbon-fiber roof lowers the vehicle's center of gravity for improved handling dynamics. Additionally, the M4 can be outfitted with carbon-ceramic brakes for enhanced durability and performance under intense driving conditions.


    Fuel Economy and Real-World MPG:

    The BMW M4 is renowned for its performance, offering fuel efficiency ratings of up to 18 miles per gallon (mpg) in urban environments and 23 mpg on highway journeys. In practical tests, specifically on our 75-mph highway fuel-economy route, the M4 equipped with a six-speed manual transmission and rear-wheel drive configuration recorded a fuel consumption of 21 mpg. This figure falls slightly below the official Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) estimates for this model. To explore detailed information regarding the BMW M4's fuel efficiency and to compare it with other vehicles, you are encouraged to visit the EPA's official website.


    Interior, Comfort, and Cargo:

    While the BMW M4 maintains the same interior layout and passenger space as its 4-series coupe counterpart, it distinguishes itself with a selection of exclusive materials and options inspired by racing. Its steering wheel, notable for its thick rim, is equipped with two red buttons that allow drivers to personalize M drive modes. The interior ambiance can be further elevated with extensive use of carbon-fiber trim and elongated paddle shifters positioned on the steering column for quick gear changes. The standard M sport seats offer significant bolstering for superior support during spirited driving and feature an illuminated logo, integrated headrests, and the option for added ventilation, ensuring both style and comfort. For those seeking an even more distinctive look and a reduction in weight, the optional carbon-fiber front seats present a striking choice. These seats are not only visually impressive but also offer additional adjustability for height, enhancing the driving experience. Although the rear seating area in the M4 does not offer as much space as the M3 sedan, it remains comfortably accommodating, dispelling any notions of discomfort. For enthusiasts who desire the freedom of open-air driving, the convertible variant of the M4 provides the ultimate experience with its retractable fabric top, offering endless headroom and the joy of the open road.
